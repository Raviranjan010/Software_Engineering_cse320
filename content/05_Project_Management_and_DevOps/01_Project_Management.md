# Project Management

## Overview
Software Project Management involves planning, scheduling, risk management, and handling resources to successfully deliver a software product within budget and time constraints.

**DEF**
**Software Project Management (SPM)**: The art and science of planning and leading software projects. It encompasses estimating scope, scheduling tasks, allocating resources, managing risks, and ensuring quality.

## the 4 P's of Project Management
Effective project management relies on the 4 P's:
1. **People**: The most important element. Managing teams, communication, and stakeholders.
2. **Product**: The software to be built. Defining the scope and requirements.
3. **Process**: The framework and activities chosen (e.g., Agile, Waterfall).
4. **Project**: All work required to make the product a reality.

## Risk Management (RMMM)
Risk management involves identifying, analyzing, and mitigating risks. 
The RMMM Plan stands for:
- **Risk Mitigation**: Avoiding the risk by taking proactive steps.
- **Risk Monitoring**: Keeping track of risk factors during the project.
- **Risk Management**: Contingency planning if the risk actually occurs.

**TIP**
Risks are generally categorized into:
- *Project Risks*: Impact budget, schedule, or personnel.
- *Technical Risks*: Impact the quality, design, and implementation.
- *Business Risks*: Impact market viability or management support.

## Task Network and WBS
- **WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)**: Breaking down the large project into smaller, manageable tasks.
- **Task Network**: Organizing the WBS tasks sequentially to show dependencies.

**★ EXAM**
The most critical part of project management is accurately estimating size and cost. A project is considered failed if it is significantly late, over budget, or lacks required features.

## Practice Questions

**Q1. According to software project management principles, which "P" is considered the most important?**
a) Product
b) People
c) Process
d) Project
**Answer: b**
*Explanation: People (the team and stakeholders) are crucial because building software is a highly human-intellectual activity.*

**Q2. What does RMMM stand for?**
a) Risk Management, Mitigation, and Monitoring
b) Risk Mitigation, Monitoring, and Management
c) Resource Mitigation, Monitoring, and Management
d) Risk Measurement, Monitoring, and Management
**Answer: b**
*Explanation: RMMM stands for Risk Mitigation, Monitoring, and Management.*

**Q3. Breaking down a project into smaller, manageable components is called:**
a) Scheduling
b) Flowcharting
c) Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
d) Object-Oriented Design
**Answer: c**
*Explanation: A Work Breakdown Structure decomposes a complex project into manageable tasks.*
