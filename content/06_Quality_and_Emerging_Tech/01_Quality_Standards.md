# Quality Standards

## Overview
Software quality ensures that the product meets the specified requirements and provides value to the user while maintaining reliability, efficiency, and maintainability. Standards guide organizations to achieve these goals systematically.

**DEF**
**Software Quality Assurance (SQA)**: The planned and systematic pattern of all actions necessary to provide adequate confidence that an item or product conforms to established technical requirements.

## 1. ISO 9001
ISO 9001 is a generic quality management standard applicable to any organization.
- Focuses on continuous improvement and customer satisfaction.
- Requires documented procedures, management commitment, and resource allocation.
- In software, ISO 9000-3 guidelines apply ISO 9001 concepts to software development.

## 2. SEI CMMI (Capability Maturity Model Integration)
CMMI evaluates the maturity of a company's software processes on a scale of 1 to 5.

**The 5 Maturity Levels**:
1. **Initial**: Chaos. Processes are ad hoc, unpredictable, and poorly controlled.
2. **Managed**: Processes are planned, performed, measured, and controlled.
3. **Defined**: Processes are well characterized and understood, standardized organization-wide.
4. **Quantitatively Managed**: Processes are measured and controlled using statistical techniques.
5. **Optimizing**: Focus on continuous process improvement based on quantitative feedback.

**★ EXAM**
**CMMI Levels**: Knowing the order (Initial -> Managed -> Defined -> Quantitatively Managed -> Optimizing) is extremely important. By Level 3 (Defined), the organization has standard sets of processes.

## 3. Six Sigma
A data-driven approach for eliminating defects. Process improvement using DMAIC:
- Define
- Measure
- Analyze
- Improve
- Control

For software development, Design for Six Sigma (DFSS) is commonly used.

## Practice Questions

**Q1. According to SEI CMMI, what is Level 3 called?**
a) Managed
b) Initial
c) Defined
d) Optimizing
**Answer: c**
*Explanation: Level 3 is "Defined", meaning processes are standardized and documented across the organization.*

**Q2. The DMAIC framework is a core component of:**
a) ISO 9001
b) Six Sigma
c) CMMI
d) Agile Scrum
**Answer: b**
*Explanation: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control (DMAIC) is fundamental to Six Sigma methodology.*

**Q3. Which standard evaluates the "Maturity" of an organization's software development process?**
a) Six Sigma
b) ISO 9000
c) PSP
d) CMMI
**Answer: d**
*Explanation: The Capability Maturity Model Integration evaluates processes across 5 levels of maturity.*
